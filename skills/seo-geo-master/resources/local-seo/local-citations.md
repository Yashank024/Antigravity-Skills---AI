# Local SEO: Local Citations

Local citations are online mentions of a business's Name, Address, and Phone number (NAP), typically found in local directories.

## 1. The Role of Citations
- They act as foundational trust signals for search engines to verify the physical existence of a business in a specific area.
- More consistent citations = Higher trust = Better Local Pack rankings.

## 2. Core Citation Targets
- **Data Aggregators**: Foursquare, Data Axle, Localeze.
- **Tier 1 Directories**: Yelp, Apple Maps, Bing Places, Facebook Business, YellowPages.
- **Niche/Industry Specific**: Avvo (Lawyers), Healthgrades (Doctors), TripAdvisor (Hotels/Restaurants).

## 3. The Consistency Rule (NAP)
- **Warning**: Inconsistent data damages trust massively.
- Example: "123 Main St." vs "123 Main Street".
- **Agent Action**: During a local audit, verify that the NAP string is 100% identical across the website footer, Google Business Profile, and Yelp. Use schema markup (`LocalBusiness`) to explicitly state the NAP on the website.
